This repository showcases quantitative simulation models developed in Python, focusing on core concepts essential to actuarial science, risk management, and financial analysis. The primary goal is to demonstrate the ability to construct stochastic models from foundational principles to solve complex probability problems and model sequential processes.

The project includes two primary types of simulations:

1.  **Monte Carlo Simulation:** Used to determine the frequentist probability of discrete events (e.g., the likelihood of obtaining a specific outcome over repeated trials, as seen in the die roll problem).
2.  **Markov Chains Modeling:** Utilized for approximating transition probabilities across successive states over time (relevant for credit risk, modeling insurance claims, or operational risk analysis).
